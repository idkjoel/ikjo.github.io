WHAT I DO

DESIGN

I work hard to make things work better.

Everything ever "designed" is the result of a series of concious decisions by the designer. So why are some things so frustrating to interact with? I do what I can to help ease the pain of interacting things that suck.

PHOTOGRAPHY

I save the moments you'd hate to forget.

Photography offers opprotunity to capture moments in time and preserve them; in the name of expression, or for the sake of remembering. A camera is a powerful tool, one that I love to experiment with.

PRINT MEDIA

I push to translate screens for fingers.

There aren't many things more satisfying than seeing work you put together come to life. I've explored a variety of types of print media and appreciate the opprotunity to hold my work in my hands.

BRANDING

I keep your identity from being a secret.

Branding is a huge part of your presence, both online and in the real world. I've worked on establishing friendly, recognizable brands for programs, departments, and companies.

ILLUSTRATION

I make art out of pixels and shapes.

Working with icon packs (specifially for iOS / macOS), my goal is to make things look better, become friendlier and maintain or improve their ease of use. You know, common sense stuff.

CHARACTER

When you look at a painting, you can often tell who's piece it is based on the design language and stylistic decisions. This character that exists in art is something I look to bring into my design work. When something is simple, beautiful, and functional, I want people to assume I had a hand in it.

PASSION

If you don't love something, set it free. Why work on projects that don't genuinely excite you? Life is too short to do work you don't truly believe in. I do my best to make sure all my projects have genuine emotions poured into them, and to be proud of the end results my projects yield.

MOTIVATION

By insisting on being personally connected to the work I do, I'm allowing myself to become invested in the end result. This investment is ultimately a motivating force that pushes me to produce the best and most effective or efficient end product.

COMFORT

A coach I had once told me that a little bit of discomfort is the key to being the best you can be -- and so I do my best to avoid the comfortable valleys that sometimes swallow us in life. That said, I believe design and interaction with things in your everyday life shouldn't be anything but comfortable.
